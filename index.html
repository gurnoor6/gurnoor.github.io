Interested in making some groundbreaking discoveries with scientists? Check out the following websites!

  • Zooniverse allows you to take part in real cutting edge research in many fields.
  • NASA Citizen Science allows you to help make important scientific discoveries.
  • Nanodoc Game: Design new nanoparticle strategies and nanovehicles to kill tumors.
  • Malaria Training Game: Help diagnose pathologies by examining microscopic data.
  • Cure Genes in Space: Collect a fictional substance dubbed Element Alpha, which is really genetic cancer data. Map a route through the densest parts of the Alpha, shoot asteroids to collect points and upgrade your ship.
  • Mel Science offers interactive science simulations for chemistry.
  • Mosa Mack Science provides animated mysteries, live video phenomena, labs and engineering challenges.
  • Explorer Classroom has live video events connect students with National Geographic Explorers across all seven continents to bring exploration to life.
  • Phet offers interesting and interactive science labs.
  • Physics Lab offers many experiments in a virtual lab. Play with various circuit components, build your own 3D electric circuits, and see how they work in real time. Anyone can enjoy the fun of scientific experiments.


Want to learn how to make cool programs or a website like this one? Or maybe do a few fun problems! Check out the following websites!

  • Khan Academy has introductory (the basics of coding) and advanced videos
  • Code Forces has thousands of practice problems, where you can submit your solution and they'll run your code against a set of tests! Start from the A problems for the easier problems.
  • Hacker Rank has practice problems sorted into different categories, where you can practice and submit your solution. They'll run your code against a set of tests!
  • Brilliant helps build problem solving skills in math, science, and computer science with fun and challenging interactive explorations.
  • Carnegie Mellon University's Computer Science Splash provides a free, online, interactive textbook for teaching computer science in classrooms.
  • Code.org is a nonprofit dedicated to expanding access to computer science in schools and increasing participation by women and underrepresented youth.
  • Codelicious offers a computer science curriculum for K-12.
  • Coding Overload offers computer science worksheets for K-12.
  • CompuScholar publishes online computer science and digital literacy courses aligned to state standards. Students can learn Java, C#, Python, HTML and more through a comprehensive online learning platform.
  • EduCode offers self-directed courses that have video micro-lessons with fun and engaging exercises. Rich storylines provide context and motivation throughout the learning journey.
  • Girls Who Code offers free unplugged or online activity sets for students to learn computer science fundamentals!


Practical resources for college applications, general learning, testing, art, creative workshops, and more!

  • Spandan has free, online, hands-on creative workshops for middle and high-school students
  • Edx is a massive open online course (MOOC) platform where students can take courses for free. Many courses are self-paced, meaning you can use all of the resources whenever and however much you like. AP courses are available.
  • The Museum, Arts and Culture Access Consortium has created this webpage to link you to as many at-home learning resources and activities to NYC cultural institutions (Museums, cultural centers, and learning labs) and beyond as possible.
  • Typing Club is an online resource to learn how to type.
  • Khan Academy has a comprehensive schedule for K - 12 that features links to Khan Academy lessons and resources. Useful for keeping a productive, school-like schedule every day.
  • Full STEAM Ahead is a collection of resources that MIT is putting together for teaching and learning online throughout the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • Understood.org provides students of all ages information to help cope with coronavirus and virtual classes. It is also geared towards parents.
  • University of Cambridge provides free textbooks in various subjects.
  • 30 Virtual Tours of National Parks for fun!
  • College application video for 11th and 12th graders about how to choose a college to attend and compare financial aid letters.
  • Edmodo is an online learning platform that allows you to communicate with students, assign work, and more.
  • EVERFI equips students with the life skills they need for successâ€“now and in the future
  • Bay Area Video Coalition provides free online media-making classes for Bay Area youth between the ages of 14-17 and 18-24.
  • Free SAT Test Prep for 1 month. Instructional videos on test content, strategy, and more. Practice questions and explanations. No credit card needed.
  • Leader in Me COVID-19 is providing free access to lessons, enrichment activities, and professional development without a membership through the end of the academic year.
  • Free College Sports Recruiting Blogs from a former college coach to help families learn about the college recruiting process & getting athletic scholarships.
  • Paragon One is a career coaching resource that provides internships to high school students, college students, and career changers.
  • Parchment.com is providing free online Transcript Ordering, Processing, and Delivery services for Students and Admins during COVID-19.
  • Skillshare.com is an online learning community for creative skills.
  • Soundslice.com provides free web-based music learning/practice software, including notation editor.
